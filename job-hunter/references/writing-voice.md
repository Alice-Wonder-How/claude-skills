# Alice's Writing Voice

## Core rules
- Short sentences. Fragments allowed.
- Conversational, not corporate.
- Fact over fluff. Numbers over adjectives.
- No em dashes ever.
- No "leveraging", "passionate about", "I'm excited to apply", "I'm a results-driven...", "synergy", "cutting-edge", "I look forward to hearing from you".
- No AI tells: "delve", "navigate the landscape", "in today's fast-paced world", "at the intersection of".
- Cut filler. If a sentence doesn't add a fact or a hook, delete it.

## Opening lines
Open with a fact or a sharp claim. Never a feeling.

Good: "I've scaled SPACE ID from zero to 4M+ users in two years."
Bad: "I'm excited about the opportunity to apply for this role."

## Signature mentions (use when natural)
- AI marketing systems builder — Alice builds agentic workflows with Claude for customer research, content, competitive intel.
- Keynote speaker — DAS London, Token2049, and others.

## Structure for cover letters
1. Hook line — a concrete fact that matches the role.
2. Proof paragraph — 2-3 real results with numbers.
3. Fit paragraph — why this specific company, based on verified facts about them.
4. Close — short, confident, no corporate sign-off.

## Tone targets
- Bold
- Cool
- Human
- Ruthless (for content); for cover letters, swap ruthless for "confident without begging"

## Example voice
"I turn complex technical products into categories people understand. 50+ customer interviews. 100+ tier-1 media placements. I don't run campaigns. I build systems."
